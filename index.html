The Metaverse/Mental Health

Space to skip

./Escapism

    One of the major affordances in using virtual reality (VR) in the metaverse is appearance of realness and how it can help someone escape the real world. As mentioned in Han et al, escapism is when someone has problems to deal with but chooses to perform other self-destructive tasks. An individual can use the metaverse as a way to escape the real world (Han et al.). The ability to have real experiences within the metaverse further promotes this idea. Self-indulgent escapism can have many side effects including depression, anxiety, and increased aggression. Furthermore, individuals will become addicted to the metaverse as they find it as a way to escape real life, causing an addiction and lower self-control (Han et al.). Other than ruining one's mental health it causes social anxiety as people will have less interactions within the real world and feel more lonely. This will further deteriorate someone's mental health and a cycle arises causing their real-life struggles to increase. Therefore, the idea of having the metaverse be real to an extent where users can use it as an escape from the real world can cause mental health to deteriorate as it becomes an addiction.

./Bullying and Harassment

    One huge problem that comes with most social media platforms is bullying, and with the metaverse this problem will become even bigger and even worse (Frenkel and Browning). When people detach from their real selves, they tend to feel like that they can do anything as they are protected by a screen or in the case of the metaverse they are protected by an avatar. This has been seen in a lot in many social media platforms and a survey done by the European commissionâ€™s joint research centre found that about 50% of children have experienced cyberbullying at least once (Enough is enough: Cyberbullying). Safety is a problem within the metaverse, and this seen in many of the VR games now which contain forms of harassment, assault, and bullying. This can become worse than social media platforms as people will be in a fully digital environment and basically be able to do anything that is done in the real world and even worse as they will think that they can easily get away with it. Moreover, One of Meta's executives, Andrew Bosworth, wrote that ''at any meaningful scale is practically impossible,'' he is mentioning how it is very hard to keep up with safety in the metaverse as it is at a very large scale. This is really concerning as even one of the biggest companies that are pushing the metaverse out into the world are unable to control safety within it. This is especially concerning for children as they will also be using the metaverse and it really hard to keep track of what people are doing. These bullying and harassment incidents can cause users to get traumatized which will affect their mental health.

The Metaverse/Physical Health

Space to skip

./Usability of The Metaverse

    The usability of the metaverse is very broad which can worsen physical health. It becomes very difficult to exercise as the usability of the metaverse is very wide and people can opt out to do activities that usually require physicality without much movement. This causes the physical health of users to deteriorate. Furthermore, some people get their physical exercise by just walking to work for example, but one thing that some corporations are trying to deploy on the metaverse is online offices, so this will further worsen physical health. This has been seen in devices such as Wii Sports where a user can play sports with their friends without having to do much physical exercise. In conclusion, the metaverse will eventually deteriorate the user's physical health if it is not used in moderation.

./Embodiment

    Embodiment also plays a big role, us as humans always want to picture the better version of ourselves, and that is what we like to post on social media specifically Instagram. We choose to show the parts of our lives that we like and hide everything else. With the metaverse we will get the opportunity to make ourselves look however we want. This forces us to live a life that is not close to our real life which is very unhealthy (Relojo-Howell). The metaverse will allow people to reach their perfect form easily which can in turn reduce physical health as people will settle for their online identity. Also, always being in the metaverse or spending long periods of time in it throughout will cause mental exhaustion. With the constant stimulation through strong lights and engaging visuals people will have a tough time recovering once their back in the real-world from this exhaustion which further affects their physical health as this will cause a constant feeling of being tired.
